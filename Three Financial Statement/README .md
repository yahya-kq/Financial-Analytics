# 📊 Accounting Cycle – Complete Financial Statements

Welcome to my **Accounting Cycle Project**!  
This project walks through a complete accounting cycle — starting from raw business transactions all the way to a full set of **Financial Statements** — built for a fictional business called **XYZ** using **Microsoft Excel**.

---

## 📌 What This Project Covers

This project demonstrates the full bookkeeping and financial reporting process:

- 📒 **Chart of Accounts** — organizing all accounts by category
- 📝 **General Journal Entries** — recording every business transaction
- 📚 **General Ledger** — posting entries and tracking balances per account
- ⚖️ **Trial Balance** — verifying that debits equal credits
- 💹 **Income Statement** — calculating revenue, expenses, and net profit
- 🏦 **Balance Sheet** — summarizing assets, liabilities, and equity
- 💵 **Cash Flow Statement** — tracking cash from operating, investing, and financing activities

## 🧠 What I Did in This Project

The project starts from zero — a new business is established with an initial capital investment. From there, every transaction is recorded as a **journal entry** using the double-entry bookkeeping system, where every debit must have a matching credit.

Those entries are then posted to individual **ledger accounts**, which are summarized into a **Trial Balance** to confirm accuracy. From the Trial Balance, three key financial statements are prepared:

- The **Income Statement** shows how much profit the business made after deducting cost of goods sold, operating expenses, and tax.
- The **Balance Sheet** shows the financial position of the business — what it owns (assets) and what it owes (liabilities), along with the owner's equity.
- The **Cash Flow Statement** breaks down cash movement into three activities: operating (day-to-day), investing (asset purchases), and financing (owner contributions or borrowing).
